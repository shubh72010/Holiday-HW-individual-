Consumer Movement

The consumer movement refers to organized efforts to protect consumers from unfair trade practices, exploitation, and to promote consumer rights and education. It involves consumer groups, activists, and government policies working together to create a safer market environment.

  • Started in the early 20th century worldwide.
  • Led to the formation of consumer protection laws and agencies.
  • Helps consumers file complaints and seek redress.
  • Raises awareness about product safety and quality.
